Welcome to my Visual Portfolio.

Environments
Pen and Ink, Oil Pastel

Environments, particularly urban and city, are one of my favourite subjects to draw. I am most attracted to artwork with a strong sense of depth and space, something that looks like I can reach in and take part in the artists' creation. When watching movies, I often find myself far more interested in the setting and the fictional world itself than in the actual characters or story. Pen and ink are my favourite medium here because the precision it offers is difficult to achieve in other media. The bottom row is a series I made as an attempt to branch out into a colored medium (oil pastels). I would love to get into matte painting.

Still Lifes
Oil and Acrylic

While my environments show my understanding of perspective, space, line, and form, these paintings show my understanding of color and lighting. The still life to the right is an array of bright, saturated objects, and the bottom left piece has warped, over-saturated colors. Often the most difficult part of painting is finding color where there is none, so the bottom middle still lives were arranged from washed out and neutral colored objects.

Illustration and Other
Pen and Ink, Oil Pastel, Oil Paint, Pencil

These are pieces that I'd like to display but don't really fall into the above categories. The bird to the right is an illustration of the second clause of "Thirteen Ways of looking at a Blackbird" by Wallace Stevens. The two pen and ink pieces below are illustrations of Joseph Conrad's Heart of Darkness. The rest are some of my more recent portraits and head studies.
