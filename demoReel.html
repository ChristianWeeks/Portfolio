Breakdown

Craving Crustacean

  • Lead rigger, animator, visual developer
  • Character designed and animated to look powerless and pathetic with his tiny hind shell, implying his sadness at the beginning
  • Small shell allowed him to emote like a dog, so he was animated as very flighty and excited

Rigging Breakdown

  • Claws and legs are hard body segments with reversible IK / FK controls
  • Body is smoothly bound to allow for smooth, exaggerated curves in spine, creating more expressive silhouettes
  • Eyes also smoothly bound. Use blend shapes for expressions, lattice controls for squash and stretch, and cluster controls along a curve in the eyestalk to control their arcs.
  • Shell is detachable and full body lattice allows for full body squash and stretch

Galaxy Simulation

  • Art directable simulation of the n-body problem run with OpenGL and accelerated in OpenCL.
  • 9216 total particles.
  • Gravity force is calculated for each particle based on the mass and distance of other particles.
  • Art direction favored over physical accuracy, so a vector field was added to nudges particles into the form of spiral galaxies most people are familiar with.
  • Paramter input files allow for different simulations by altering features like the particle's initial position, the gravitational constant, and the strength of the vector field.

Hades Head

Responsible for all assets except the audio, which is taken from Disney's Hercules. I chose this particular sound segment because its potential for very expressive animation along with experimentation in fire effects. Rigging is done mostly with joints on control curves with blend shapes.
